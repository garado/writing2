Reflection
This project taught me a lot about my own writing. I realized that I only like writing when my natural voice and style aren't suppressed - part of why I hated high school writing classes so much is because I had to write in the cookie-cutter style that the AP exam graders would like. I appreciated this project and this class because I didn't have to do that at all. (Well, except for the Fancy Formal Academic Research Papers, but even then, that was about a topic I really liked, so I didn't mind.) It re-sparked that love of writing that I used to have when I was younger.
By doing this project, I also discovered the real reason behind my crippling writer's block. It's caused by a fear of failure and intense perfectionism. (Shoutout to Anne Lamott for helping me realize that.) The Shitty First Drafts article really opened my eyes and taught me that literally everyone fucking sucks when they start writing and that there isn't a single great writer alive that writes like Shakespeare on their first try. I learned that it's okay to suck! In the words of Jake the Dog from Adventure Time, "sucking at something is the first step to being sort of good at something." This is a lesson that I can carry with me and apply to the rest of my life as well.
Overall, this writing development journal helped me achieve the main goal that I set for myself at the beginning of the quarter, which was to rediscover how much I liked writing. I also greatly improved my annoying lil writer's block weakness. Another unexpected thing that I learned was that I get extremely motivated to do stuff when I tie things into my personal hobbies/interests. I put off this WDJ for a while until I got the idea to make a really aesthetic website for it. I spent a couple hours coding the website from scratch, which finally got me to stop procrastinating on it. I'm definitely still missing some entries here and there, but at least it's pretty.
