Reflection
One. My writing process was kind of shitty before I took Writing 2. In the past, I would spend hours trying to deal with writer's block and I would have immense difficulty just getting something onto the paper. I would end up just staring at a blank Google document and have only a few sentences written after a couple of hours. This class has helped me get over a lot of that! Reading Lamott's "Shitty First Drafts" article helped me recognize my inability to start writing for what it secretly was - a fear of failure. Now, when I start papers, I literally just write whatever bullshit springs into mind. I did exactly that for my Project D first draft and I managed to write three pages in an hour - they were shitty pages, but they were pages!
Two. My greatest strengths as a writer are my knowledge of correct grammar/writing conventions.
Three. My relationship with writing improved, which was one of my goals at the beginning of the quarter.
Four. Yikes! I'm not done with Project B, C, or D.
